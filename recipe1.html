Indonesian Fried Rice

My mother’s family is from Indonesia and this recipe was a cheap staple meal that has evolved into what it is today through years of trial and error.

Serves: 8

Ingredients:










Instructions:

  1. Cook the 4 cups of white rice according to package directions.
    • Cooking time and ratio of water to rice may vary depending on the method used (ie stovetop, rice cooker etc.).
  2. Dice the sausage links and onions.
    • To ensure that the sausage links do not turn into ground sausage, freeze the links before attempting to dice.
  3. Place both types of sausage and the onions in a large skillet over medium heat and cook until well done.
  4. Add the garlic, bullion, sambal and water and mix until bullion is dissolved.
  5. Sprinkle generous amounts of paprika over the skillet, mixing and adding more until it reaches the desired shade of red.
  6. Add Better than bouillon, sambal, water, and paprika. Stir well to combine all ingredients.
  7. Keep uncovered and allow to simmer until the liquid turns into a sauce.
  8. Thoroughly mix the rice and sauce to serve.
  9. Can be served with scrambled eggs, kroepoek and/or sugared tomatoes for a more complex flavor.
    • To make the sugared tomatoes, dice tomatoes and cover with sugar 2-3 hours before serving.
